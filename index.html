Common questions

The short answers.

When do cohorts run?
Seasonally, twice a year. The Summer cohort runs June through August and is in progress now. The Winter cohort runs January through March — applications are open, and spots are limited by design: we only take on what our mentors can stand behind.
What does it cost?
Flat-fee projects priced for small businesses — a fraction of typical consulting rates, with no hiring costs and no long-term commitment. We'll give you a real number in the first conversation, before you commit to anything.
Who can become an apprentice?
Recent graduates and early-career people, selected by application. We screen for communication, curiosity, and drive — not a specific degree. Every apprentice completes Bloom's training before their first engagement.
What kind of work do apprentices actually do?
Hands-on AI implementation, directed by a senior mentor: automating rote workflows, building AI-assisted processes, and documenting everything so your team can run it without us. You approve the project scope before anyone starts.

We're not automating people out of the workforce.

We're using AI to bring more people in — and leaving both sides better equipped than when we started. Our Winter cohort is forming now: a small group of Bay Area businesses, apprentices, and senior mentors, starting in January.
